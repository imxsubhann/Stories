One hot afternoon, a hungry fox was walking through a vineyard. He saw a bunch of juicy, ripe grapes hanging high on a vine.

The fox jumped as high as he could, but he could not reach the grapes. He tried again and again, but every time he failed.

After many attempts, the tired fox gave up. As he walked away, he said, "Those grapes are probably sour anyway. I don't want them."

The fox left without eating the grapes, even though he really wanted them.

Moral: It is easy to dislike something you cannot have.
